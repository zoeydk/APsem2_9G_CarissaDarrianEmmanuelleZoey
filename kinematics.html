Displacement

Speed, Distance, Time
Time is how long a motion takes, can be in seconds (s), minutes (m) or hours (h). For example, running for 10 minutes or driving for 2 hours. Distance is how much ground you covered, can be in meters (m) or kilometers (km). For example, walking 5 meters forward and then 3 meters back. Speed is how fast something moves, it can be in meters per second (m/s) or kilometers per hour (km/h). For example, the speed of a car is moving at a speed of 72 km/h.

Newton’s laws:
First = Any object will stay at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon (inertia) Second = Acceleration of an object is linked to the force acting on it (a = F / m) Third = Every action has an equal and opposite reaction

Distance, Time graph
The horizontal/x axis is the time The vertical/y axis is the distance

Graph line
Straight Slanted Line: The object is moving at a constant speed Flat Line: Object is not moving at all Curved Line: The object’s speed is changing, either speeding up or slowing down

Acceleration and Velocity
The speed and direction an object is moving is called velocity. Making an object speed up or slow down is called acceleration or deceleration.
