Thermal Energy

Thermal energy is the total kinetic energy of particles (atoms/molecules) in a system, depending on mass and speed. Heat is the transfer of energy due to a temperature difference, moving from hotter to cooler bodies.

Methods of heat transfer:

Conduction: Transfer through direct contact.
Convection: Transfer by fluid movement.
Radiation: Transfer by electromagnetic waves.

Thermal properties:

Specific heat capacity: Amount of energy needed to change the temperature of a substance (1kg) by 1°C.
Latent heat: Amount of energy needed to change the phase of a substance (melting, boiling, or evaporation).
Thermal expansion: The increase in a material’s length, area, or volume due to rising temperatures.

Formula:
Length change (m) = Initial length (m) × CTE × Temperature change

Key factors influencing thermal expansion:

Different materials expand at different rates. Metals generally have higher expansion coefficients than ceramics or plastics.

A greater increase in temperature causes greater expansion, while a decrease causes contraction.

A longer rod or larger volume will experience a greater total change in length or volume compared to a smaller one, assuming the same material.

Higher pressure usually reduces the amount of thermal expansion in liquids.

Pressure formula:
P1 / T1 = P2 / T2

P = Pressure
T = Temperature
