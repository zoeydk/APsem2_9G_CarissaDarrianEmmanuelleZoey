Static Electricity

In the video, it was shown that my friend rubbed a plastic sheet with a piece of cloth and then used the plastic sheet to pull a metallic can toward it. This demonstration illustrates the concept of static electricity. When the plastic sheet was wiped with the cloth, friction caused electrons to transfer from one material to the other, leaving the plastic sheet electrically charged. As a result, the plastic gained a static charge.

When the charged plastic sheet was brought near the metallic can, it caused the charges inside the can to rearrange due to electrostatic induction. The side of the can closest to the plastic became oppositely charged, creating an attractive force between the two objects. This force was strong enough to move the can, even though it was initially neutral.
